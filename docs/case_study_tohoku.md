# Case Study, Tohoku 2011

## Objective

This document provides a simple historical illustration of how a systemic instability framework may be used to interpret large seismic transitions.

It is not intended as a deterministic prediction claim.

## Event

- Event: Tohoku earthquake
- Date: 2011-03-11
- Magnitude: 9.0
- Region: Japan trench area

## Conceptual interpretation

A systemic framework such as CGIE would not aim to predict the exact event.
Instead, it would look for:

- rising regional clustering
- increasing event density
- persistence of elevated activity
- transition from stable to pressure or instability regime

## Why this case matters

The Tohoku event is useful as a retrospective example because it allows readers to understand what a transition regime could look like in practice.

## Positioning

This case study is illustrative.
It is included to explain the logic of systemic instability detection, not to claim operational predictive success.
