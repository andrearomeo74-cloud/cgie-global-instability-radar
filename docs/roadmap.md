# Roadmap

## Current stage

The current repository represents the public prototype layer of the Congruity Global Instability Engine.

The system has reached a stable baseline:

**V48 Alert Engine**

This version provides the best balance between:

, precision  
, lead time  
, number of alerts  
, operational usability  

The project is now transitioning from exploratory testing to structured development.

## Short term objectives

### 1. Baseline consolidation
Stabilize and document the current system:

, clean implementation of V48  
, reproducible pipeline  
, standardized input/output format  
, consistent reporting layer  

### 2. Parameter exploration
Improve performance through controlled tuning:

, threshold optimization  
, window size analysis  
, signal filtering strategies  
, sensitivity vs precision trade off  

### 3. Single variable extensions
Introduce additional physical variables incrementally:

, energy only models  
, depth variation models  
, spatial dispersion models  

Each variable will be tested independently before integration.

## Medium term objectives

### 4. Multi region validation
Test the system across different seismic contexts:

, volcanic systems  
, tectonic fault zones  
, low activity regions  

This will evaluate generalization capability.

### 5. Temporal regime analysis
Study system behavior across different regimes:

, quiet periods  
, transitional phases  
, high activity clusters  

This will help refine phase classification logic.

### 6. Data enrichment
Integrate additional data sources:

, higher resolution seismic feeds  
, regional datasets  
, auxiliary geophysical indicators  

## Long term objectives

### 7. Real time pipeline
Build a continuous processing system:

, automated data ingestion  
, daily or hourly updates  
, continuous signal generation  

### 8. Alerting system
Develop an operational alert layer:

, configurable thresholds  
, notification system  
, monitoring interface  

### 9. Visualization and dashboard
Extend the public radar into a full interface:

, interactive dashboard  
, temporal playback  
, multi layer visualization  

### 10. System integration
Position the engine as a component within larger systems:

, decision support tools  
, monitoring platforms  
, research infrastructures  

## Research direction

The long term research direction is focused on:

, understanding seismic activity as a dynamic system  
, identifying phase transitions rather than isolated events  
, extracting weak but persistent anticipatory signals  

The goal is not deterministic prediction, but:

**structured interpretation of complex system behavior**

## Open questions

Key questions guiding future work:

, how stable are the detected patterns across regions  
, what variables improve signal quality without overfitting  
, what is the optimal balance between precision and lead  
, how to validate weak signals in noisy systems  

## Final vision

The project aims to evolve into:

, a global instability observatory  
, a temporal pattern detection engine  
, a phase transition monitoring system  

capable of transforming raw seismic data into meaningful, interpretable system level insights.
